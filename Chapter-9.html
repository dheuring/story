Chapter 9

Running the Show: Two Large Training Projects

(1997-2009)

My most intensive phase of management involved developing and delivering two massive, multi-year, statewide training programs, defining my late career at the DHS.

Following my leadership experience at the OEO, I transitioned into a role that would dominate the next decade of my career at the Minnesota DHS: managing and delivering massive, statewide training programs. My anthropological training and experience in Saudi Arabia, which emphasized organizational analysis and cross-cultural communication, provided an ideal foundation for this demanding work. This period saw me direct two of the largest training and change management initiatives the agency had undertaken.

The first project involved training thousands of state and county staff across Minnesota on a new eligibility determination system. This wasn't just technical training; it required a complete cultural shift in how caseworkers approached their jobs. My team was responsible for developing all curriculum, delivering hundreds of training sessions, and managing the complex logistics of a statewide rollout. Success hinged on effective communication and change management, fields where my background proved invaluable.

The second, equally challenging project was the **Minnesota Child Safety and Permanency Training System**, a multi-year effort to professionalize the state‚Äôs child protection workforce. This endeavor was even more complex, requiring deep collaboration with academic partners, county social services, and community advocates. The goal was to create a consistent, competency-based curriculum for all new and existing child protection workers‚Äîa critical measure for improving outcomes for vulnerable children.

Serving as the project director for both initiatives, I was essentially "running the show" for training, managing large budgets, complex contracts, and large teams of trainers and subject matter experts. It was a time of immense professional achievement and satisfaction, solidifying my reputation as a manager capable of leading large-scale organizational change. The lessons learned about project management, stakeholder engagement, and team dynamics during these years would ultimately pave the way for my retirement and my transition into a new phase of life as an author and independent consultant.
