The final twelve years of my public service career, culminating in my retirement in 2021, represented my **"capstone"** period. Having successfully led major training initiatives (as detailed in Chapter 9), I transitioned into roles focused on high-level policy, legislative action, and strategic management within the Minnesota Department of Human Services (DHS).

As a senior manager, my responsibilities shifted from direct program implementation to shaping the environment in which those programs operated. I worked closely with legislators, county governments, and federal partners, synthesizing complex social issues into clear policy recommendations. This phase required utilizing my full range of skills: the analytical clarity of an anthropologist, the organizational knowledge of a Saudi expat, and the leadership capacity of a seasoned director. I was often viewed as a **"policy translator,"** bridging the gap between political intent and administrative reality.

Beyond work, this decade was also defined by personal fulfillment. Our children, Jessica and Anna, grew into young adults, and Pam and I cherished the stability we had built. The flexibility gained from my seniority allowed me to pursue personal interests, notably the writing of this memoir and the deepening of my long-term interest in audio production and sound engineering.

My retirement in 2021 was a deliberate choice, not an end point, but a pivot. It concluded a 35-year public sector career built on the foundation of education and diverse international and domestic experiences. I left DHS with a profound sense of accomplishment, ready to dedicate my time fully to independent projects‚Äîspecifically, the public sharing of my life story through this memoir and its audio companion, allowing the next stage of my plan to truly begin.
