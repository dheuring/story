Chapter 6

First Step of the Plan: Building the Foundation

(1979-1984)

The 1980s were about building stability: graduating college, getting married, and starting a career.

The 1980s began with me back at the University of Minnesota, focused on Cultural Anthropology. This time, I was driven, utilizing the self-discipline forged during my Air Force years and the clarity gained during my time in Thailand. I graduated in 1980, the first person in my family to earn a college degree, and immediately began the master's program. My academic pursuit felt both necessary and fulfilling‚ÄîI was finally on the right path.

However, life outside the classroom quickly took precedence. My personal life took a decisive turn when I met my future wife, Pam, through a mutual friend. Our relationship blossomed quickly; we were married in 1981, and our daughter, Jessica, was born in 1983. This whirlwind of commitment meant adjusting my career plans. Academic jobs in Cultural Anthropology were scarce, and the responsibility of family prompted a pragmatic shift: I needed a steady job.

My first true career step involved a year-long internship with the Minnesota Department of Human Services (DHS). I excelled, finding satisfaction in applying my anthropological training to social issues, particularly in working with tribal communities. The internship led to a full-time position as a research analyst, effectively launching my 28-year career in the public sector.

This period marked the "First Step of the Plan"‚Äîthe plan being an unwritten, evolving roadmap for a life combining professional stability with personal happiness. I had secured a foundation: a good job, a loving family, and a home. The excitement of foreign travel had been replaced by the quiet, daily joy of building a life with Pam and Jessica. While my formal international travel ended for a time, my anthropological curiosity remained the engine of my work, driving me to understand and connect with diverse communities right here in Minnesota. I was ready for the next step‚Äîa career path that would soon lead me back to international work in a completely unexpected capacity.
